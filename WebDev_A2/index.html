About Hamsters

Hamsters are small rodents belonging to the subfamily Cricetinae.They are native to parts of Europe and Asia. These adorable creaturesare popular as pets due to their small size and cute appearance.

Types Of Hamsters

If you're thinking about getting a hamster, these adorable little creatures make fun, entertaining pets. There are 24 hamster species, however, five are the most commonly kept as pets. Some hamster species make perfect solo pets, while others are comfortable living in a group. If you're looking for a hamster to hold and interact with, some breeds are better suited for handling while others prefer to play on their own.

Essential Items For Hamsters

Although hamsters are easy and low maintenance pets, there are various important items a hamster needs to have in order to be happy and well taken care of.

Bedding

Bedding is important as it allows for thr hamster to naturally burrow and create comfortable spaces, it is also used to absorb the urine of the hamster. Popular and effective bedding includes, Paaper Litter, Aspen Shavings, Paper Pellets, Thimothy Hay

Sand Bath

They love rolling, digging, and even playing in smooth, clean, sand as it helps “bathe” their skin and coat in a non-traditional way. Sand baths help decrease natural oils and dirt that may have accumulated in your hamster's coat. They may also deter parasites.22

Hideouts

Your hamster may hide in a corner to sleep, burrow or if it is stressed, but have you asked yourself why it chooses to hide in the corner of its cage. The answer is simple really. Corners provide more safety and as prey animals they instinctively look to protect themselves. .

Hamster Wheel

In their natural environment, hamsters live in elaborate underground burrowing systems, constantly scurrying from one end to another. Exercise wheels help provide domesticated hamsters with aerobic activity to mimic this behavior. Some hamsters will run up to five miles a night on their exercise wheels.
